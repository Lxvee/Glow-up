Facial Grooming
The Face
Protocol
Zone-by-zone grooming guide. Every detail composes your overall aesthetic.
👁️
Eye Area
Undereye · Lids · Expression
▾
1
Cold Spoon Depuff
Leave two metal spoons in the fridge overnight. In the morning, press the curved side gently under each eye for 60 seconds. Dramatically reduces under-eye puffiness before anyone sees you.
Every AM
2
Under-Eye Tap
Use your ring finger (weakest, least pressure) to gently tap your Cetaphil or jojoba oil under the orbital bone. Never drag — the skin here is 0.5mm thick. Tap from inner to outer corner.
PM only
3
Eye Exercise — Focus Shift
Look far away for 20 seconds, then at your finger close up for 20 seconds. Repeat 5 times. Reduces eye strain that causes squinting lines. Do while brushing teeth.
Daily
4
Sleep Position
Back sleeping prevents under-eye fluid pooling and pillow crease lines. If you're a side sleeper, use a satin or silk pillowcase — cotton creates friction that ages skin faster.
Nightly
〰️
Eyebrows
Shape · Symmetry · Fullness
▾
1
Daily Brush — Upward & Outward
Use a clean spoolie every morning. Brush brow hairs upward, then outward. This trains growth direction over weeks and makes brows look fuller and more defined without product.
Every AM
2
Castor Oil at Night
Dip a spoolie or fingertip into castor oil and apply through both brows before bed. Ricinoleic acid stimulates follicles. Results visible in 3–6 weeks with consistency. Fills sparse areas over time.
Nightly
3
Shape Maintenance
Only pluck single hairs below the natural arch — never reshape the top line. Clean up the space between brows (mono-brow area) every 5–7 days. For the best arch, let a professional shape once, then maintain that line.
Weekly
✧
Lashes
Length · Curl · Growth
▾
1
Castor Oil on Lash Line
Same castor oil you use for brows. Apply to the lash line (not the lashes themselves) with a clean spoolie or cotton bud every night. Promotes growth and thickness over 4–6 weeks.
Nightly
2
Lash Curl — Heated Spoon Method
Warm a clean metal spoon in hot water, dry it, then press gently against your lashes for 10 seconds per eye. Creates a natural curl without a curler. Do before any other eye look.
When styled
3
Don't Rub Your Eyes
Rubbing pulls lashes out at the root and damages follicles permanently. If you feel the urge, gently blink or press (don't rub) a cool finger over closed lids. This is one of the most important lash habits.
Always
👄
Lips
Texture · Hydration · Definition
▾
1
Sugar Scrub — 2× per week
Mix ½ tsp sugar + few drops of honey on your fingertip. Gently massage lips in circles for 30 seconds. Rinse. Removes dead skin, restores natural definition of lip line. Do before moisturising lips at night.
2× weekly
2
Vaseline or Honey at Night
Apply a thick layer to lips every night before sleep. Lips have no sebaceous glands — they can't moisturise themselves. Overnight occlusion heals cracks and keeps them plump. Raw honey also works beautifully.
Nightly
3
Stop Licking Your Lips
Saliva evaporates instantly and takes lip moisture with it. Licking is the primary cause of chronic dry, dark, or peeling lips. Reach for lip balm instead — always have one on you.
Always
4
Sun Protection for Lips
Lips are one of the first areas to show hyperpigmentation and darkening from UV exposure. Use a lip balm with SPF during the day, or apply a small amount of your face SPF to the lips.
Daily

Hair Care
1b/1c Hair
Protocol
Straight to wavy hair — your sea salt spray and clay are perfect tools. Here's exactly how to use them.
Your Hair Type — 1b/1c Straight with a slight natural wave or bend. Fine to medium texture. Goal: enhance natural texture, add volume, reduce frizz, keep it looking intentionally styled rather than flat or limp.
🚿
Wash Day Routine
2–3× per week · Not daily
▾
1
Pre-Wash Oil Treatment (optional but powerful)
Apply your jojoba oil to dry hair — roots to ends — 30 minutes before washing. This pre-poo protects the hair shaft from shampoo stripping. Particularly good for 1c hair that goes frizzy after washing.
Wash days
2
Shampoo — Scalp Only
Apply shampoo to scalp only. Do not pile hair on head — this causes tangles and breakage. The shampoo rinsing through the lengths is enough to cleanse them. Massage scalp for 2 minutes — this stimulates follicles for growth.
2–3× weekly
3
Conditioner — Mid-Lengths to Ends
Never apply conditioner to roots — it weighs 1b/1c hair flat. Apply mid-length to ends, leave for 2–3 minutes, then rinse with cool water (closes the cuticle for shine). Wide-tooth comb through while wet.
Every wash
4
Microfibre Towel or T-Shirt Dry
Never rub hair with a regular towel — the rough fibres cause frizz and breakage. Scrunch gently with a microfibre towel or old cotton t-shirt. Let hair air dry 80% before any heat styling.
Every wash
🌊
Sea Salt Spray — How to Use It
Your texture tool
▾
1
Apply to Damp, Not Wet Hair
Sea salt spray works best at 60–70% dry. On soaking wet hair it just dilutes. On fully dry hair it can make it crunchy and dull. That damp-but-not-dripping window is the target.
Key timing
2
Scrunch, Don't Smooth
Spray sections and scrunch upward toward the scalp — this encourages your natural 1c wave to form. Smoothing downward will just make it flat. Let it air dry scrunched for maximum texture.
Technique
3
Refresh Dry Hair
On non-wash days, lightly mist sea salt spray on dry hair and scrunch to revive texture and volume. Works better than dry shampoo for 1b/1c hair that goes flat. Avoid the roots to prevent buildup.
Non-wash days
4
Limit to 4–5 Uses Per Week
Sea salt is mildly drying. Daily use without proper conditioning will make hair brittle over time. Alternate with conditioning leave-in on other days. Balance is key.
Max 4–5×
🏺
Clay — How to Use It
Your control tool
▾
1
Emulsify First — Always
Rub a pea-sized amount between your palms until it's warm and slightly tacky. Never apply directly from the tub — you'll get uneven clumps. The warmth from your hands activates the hold.
Technique
2
Apply to Dry or Slightly Damp
For 1b hair: apply to dry hair for maximum control and matte finish. For 1c texture: apply to slightly damp hair so it works with the wave. Work from the ends first, then the mid-lengths, roots last (use the least here).
Daily or as needed
3
Style Looks You Can Do
Textured, separated look: scrunch and tousle with fingers. Sleek pushed-back: use a comb after applying. Defined pieces: pull individual sections to separate. Clay gives you lived-in texture without looking greasy — the matte finish is key for 1b/1c.
Style

Scalp Health
The foundation of great hair
▾
1
Scalp Massage — 5 Min Daily
Use fingertips (not nails) to massage scalp in circular motions for 5 minutes daily — can be done dry or during shower. Increases blood flow to follicles. Studies show consistent scalp massage thickens hair shaft diameter over 6 months.
Every day
2
Cold Water Rinse — Final Step
After every wash, end with 30 seconds of cold water over your hair. This closes the cuticle (the outer layer of each hair strand), making it lay flat and reflect light — the literal definition of shine.
Every wash
3
Overnight Jojoba on Ends
Once a week: apply 1–2 drops of jojoba to the last 2 inches of your hair only before sleep. Prevents split ends and breakage. Put hair in a loose braid or bun so it doesn't transfer to your pillow (or use a satin pillowcase).
Weekly
Posture & Presence
Neck &
Bearing
Posture defines how you're perceived before you say a word. A strong neck and upright bearing communicates confidence at a biological level.
Why Neck Training Matters for Aesthetics A well-developed neck creates the illusion of a narrower face, stronger jaw definition, and an overall more composed appearance. Forward head posture (from phones) ages faces by distorting proportions. These exercises fix that.
△
AM Neck Routine
5 minutes · Every morning
▾
Chin Tucks
3 × 10 reps
Stand or sit tall. Draw your chin straight back (making a "double chin") without tilting your head up or down. Hold 2 seconds. Release. This retracts the cervical spine and counters forward head posture directly.
Feel it: you should feel a stretch at the base of your skull, not in your throat.
```
Neck Rotations
10 each side
Slowly turn head to the right as far as comfortable. Hold 2 seconds. Return to center. Repeat left. No jerking — controlled, deliberate movement. Maintains range of motion and prevents stiffness.
Do not roll the neck in a full circle — this can strain the cervical joints.
Lateral Neck Stretch
30 sec each side
Drop right ear toward right shoulder. Place right hand gently on the left side of your head — don't pull, just rest the weight. Feel the stretch along the left side of your neck. Switch sides.
Never yank the head — the weight of your hand alone is enough.
Neck Isometrics
4 directions × 5 sec hold
Place palm against forehead. Push head forward while resisting with palm — don't move. Hold 5 sec. Repeat with palm on back of head (press backward), then each side. This builds neck strength without movement, reducing injury risk.
Use 40–60% of your maximum force. This is strength building, not a contest.
```

Posture Throughout Day
How you carry yourself constantly
▾
1
Phone Position
Every 10° your head tilts forward adds 10 lbs of pressure to your cervical spine. Hold your phone at eye level, not lap level. This single habit prevents "tech neck" — the premature sagging of skin under the jawline.
2
The Wall Test — Daily Reset
Stand with heels, glutes, shoulders, and back of head all touching a wall. This is optimal posture. Hold for 60 seconds then walk away — this programs your proprioceptive sense of "upright." Do once every morning.
3
Jaw Position — Mewing
Rest your entire tongue (not just tip) against the roof of your mouth. Lips together, teeth lightly touching or just apart, breathing through nose. This is the anatomically correct jaw resting position and defines jawline and mid-face over months.
4
Shoulders Down and Back
Every time you walk through a doorway, reset: pull shoulders back, drop them away from your ears, lift through the crown. Make it a ritual trigger. Doorway = posture check.
5
Neck Moisturise
Extend every skincare product down to your neck and décolletage. The neck shows aging faster than the face because it's always ignored in routines. Niacinamide and Cetaphil on the neck — every AM and PM, always.

Grooming Extras
The details that elevate everything
▾
1
Teeth — Oil Pulling
Swish 1 tsp of coconut or sesame oil in mouth for 5–10 minutes each morning before brushing. Ancient Ayurvedic practice shown to reduce oral bacteria, whiten teeth naturally, and improve gum health. Do while making coffee or scrolling.
Before brushing
2
Teeth — Brush Technique
2 minutes, twice daily. Electric toothbrush is significantly better than manual. Use circular motions at 45° to the gumline. Brush tongue too — this removes 90% of bad breath bacteria. Floss nightly.
AM + PM
3
Hands & Nails
Clean, trimmed nails are one of the most noticed grooming details. File weekly, push back cuticles with a damp towel. Moisturise hands with any leftover Cetaphil after your face routine. Keep nails short and consistent in shape.
Weekly shaping
4
Fragrance
Applied to pulse points: wrists, neck, behind ears, inner elbow. Apply after moisturiser — the hydration helps fragrance last longer. A signature scent is one of the most powerful and memorable aesthetic elements. It precedes and outlasts your visual presence.
